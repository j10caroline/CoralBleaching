These resources all depend on coral.
Without coral's unique habitat, they will disappear.
Coral reefs are not dead yet, but if trends continue,
90% of reefs will die by 2050.

Healthy

Healthy coral maintains a symbiotic relationship with zooxanthellae algae, which provides food and bright colors, inside the coral’s tissues.

Stressed

When the sea surface temperatures rise, ocean circulation patterns change. Cold and warm tides move around more and temperatures fluctuate greatly. This stress causes algae to leave.

Bleached

If the stress is prolonged, the coral turns white and is susceptible to disease. There is a very slim chance that the algae could return. Over half of the world’s coral is considered bleached.

Dead

In 2016, an estimated 29% of reefs worldwide were dead. Dead corals crumble and fall apart, no longer retaining wildlife habitats, coastline protection, or breathtaking tourism.
